6. THE THOMAS CLAUSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Thomas is a small teddy bear. He first appeared as a hidden Easter egg inside
Picasa, Google's now-discontinued photo management software, placed there in
appreciation for Noah Grey -- pioneering photoblogger, creator of Greymatter,
and one of the people who invented what the web became when people used it to
share their lives and photographs with strangers who cared. Thomas was an act
of love, and a surprise.

When Google ended Picasa, Thomas disappeared with it. This caused Noah Grey
genuine grief, as well it should have. Thomas was subsequently incorporated
into the SNAPSMACK codebase as a hidden Easter egg, in the same spirit --
as a tribute to Noah Grey, as an act of continuity, and because small acts
of love hidden inside software are exactly the kind of thing SNAPSMACK is for.

Noah Grey saw Thomas in SNAPSMACK. He had a happy cry. He showed his sister.
She had a happy cry too. This clause exists to make sure Thomas is never
lost again.

6.1  REQUIREMENTS

     Any Fork must retain the Thomas Easter egg in a form that is:

     (a) Functionally present -- Thomas must be findable by someone
         looking for him.

     (b) Attributed to its origin -- the Easter egg must credit Noah Grey
         by name as the photoblogging pioneer in whose honour Thomas was
         originally placed in Picasa.

     (c) Substantially intact -- Thomas may be reskinned or redrawn to
         suit a Fork's aesthetic, but he must remain a bear, he must
         remain hidden, and he must remain a small act of love.

     The following attribution language, or language substantially
     equivalent in meaning and spirit, must appear in the Easter egg's
     reveal or accompanying documentation:

         Thomas is a teddy bear. He first appeared as a hidden Easter egg
         in Picasa, Google's photo software, placed there in appreciation
         for Noah Grey -- photoblogging pioneer, creator of Greymatter,
         and one of the people who built what the web was supposed to be.
         When Picasa ended, Thomas disappeared. He lives here now.
         He is not going anywhere.

6.2  CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION

     Any party who removes Thomas from a Fork, strips his attribution,
     renders him unfindable, or otherwise acts to erase him from the
     lineage of this software shall be subject to the following:

     (a) Flaming ass herpes. The severity, duration, and precise
         anatomical distribution of said flaming ass herpes shall be at
         the sole and non-reviewable discretion of the original author.
         No appeal process exists. No cure shall be forthcoming. The
         violating party will have earned this.

     (b) The violating party shall be required to reflect, at length, on
         what they have done and why it was wrong. They should think about
         Noah Grey. They should think about the people who hid Thomas in
         Picasa. They should think about what it means to erase small acts
         of love from the historical record. This reflection is not
         optional.

     (c) The legal enforceability of Section 6.2(a) in any given
         jurisdiction is left as an exercise for the violating party's
         attorney. It is included not because it is enforceable but
         because it is correct.

6.3  THE SPIRIT OF THIS CLAUSE

     SNAPSMACK exists because the platforms that were supposed to serve
     artists decided to serve themselves instead. Thomas exists in
     SNAPSMACK because the software that was supposed to keep things safe
     let them disappear.

     Thomas is a small teddy bear. He has survived the end of Picasa.
     He will survive whatever comes next, because this clause says so,
     and because anyone who would remove him deserves what Section 6.2(a)
     promises.

     Thomas was first hidden in Picasa in appreciation for Noah Grey,
     creator of Greymatter and photoblogging pioneer. He lives in
     SNAPSMACK now. He is not going anywhere.
